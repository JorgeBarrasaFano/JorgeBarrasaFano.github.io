Welcome to my personal space, in which I share all the research-related stuff I do, as well as my science communication activities.

My work focuses on applying image analysis and other computational techniques to help biologists understand in a quantitative way what is going on within their in vitro experiments with cells.

Currently, I am based at the Mechanobiology and Tissue Engineering Lab at KU Leuven, Belgium. Here, our primary focus lies in exploring the biomechanics underlying blood vessel growth, particularly within the context of vascular diseases.

Beyond my professional pursuits, I am deeply passionate about science communication. I frequently share insights into my work and that of my peers on Twitter. Additionally, I serve as the Head of the Science Communication Committee at the Spanish Scientists in Belgium association. In this role, I organize various science communication events in both Spanish and English, and contribute to the science segment of a local radio program, Radioalma.

Moreover, I am actively involved in the Science Communication Committee of the European Society of Biomechanics (ESB). Here, I lead journal clubs and contribute to the organization of annual conferences.

If you share an interest in data analysis within in vitro systems or are curious about science communication, I invite you to explore my website further. Welcome!
