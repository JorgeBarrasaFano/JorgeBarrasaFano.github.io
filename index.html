Welcome to my personal space, in which I share all the research-related stuff I do, as well as my science communication activities.

My work focuses on applying image analysis and other computational techniques to help biologists understand in a quantitative way what is going on within their experiments with cells.

I do this work in the lab of Mechanobiology and Tissue Engineering at KU Leuven, in Belgium. Here we mainly focus on studying the biomechanics that take place during blood vessel growth, particlularly in the context of vascular diseases.

My passion about science goes beyond what I do at work, which made me dive into the world of science communication. I regularly share my work and that of other colleagues that I find interesting on Twitter. Moreover, I am the Head of the Science Communication Committee of the Spanish Scientists in Belgium association. Here I organize several science communication events in Spanish and in English as well as collaborate in the science section of a radio program of the local radio station Radioalma. Finally, I am also part of the Science Communication Committee of the European Society of Biomechanics (ESB). Here, I organize journal clubs, and help in the organization of the anual conferences.
